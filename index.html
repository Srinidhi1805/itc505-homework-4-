This section details the rationale behind the chosen HTML structure for the academic paper "Impact of Social Media On Youth". Each choice is carefully curated to ensure semantic relevance, improve accessibility, and enhance the overall readability of the document.

Title of the Paper

The title "Impact of Social Media On Youth" uses the <h1> tag, which denotes the main title of a document or a section of a document. As this tag represents the top level heading, it is semantically correct to use it for the paper's title.

Author Details

The author name uses the <h2> tag, signifying its importance as the second highest level of heading after the title. The <address> tag wraps the department, university, location, and email details. This tag is specifically intended for marking up contact information. The email address is enclosed in an <a> tag with a "mailto:" href attribute, making it a clickable email link.

Sections and Subsections

Sections like "Abstract", "Introduction", "Literature Review", and so forth, are wrapped in individual <section> elements to semantically group content together. Each section's title is represented by an <h2> tag, ensuring clear hierarchy and improving navigation. The subsections within these sections use <h3>tags (we didn't have sub-sub sections to h3 suffices) ensuring hierarchical and logical representation of headings and subheadings. The paragraphs under the headings are, for obvious reasons, surrouned by the <p> tags.

Paragraphs and Content

Regular text content within each section is wrapped in <p> tags, which denote paragraphs. This ensures proper spacing and distinction between distinct blocks of text, improving readability.

References

The "References" section employs an ordered list <ol> with each reference enclosed in a <li> tag. This structure is used because academic references are often sequentially numbered. Each entry in the bibliography has a clear order, making an ordered list the most semantically accurate choice.

Additional Stylistic or Semantic Elements

Additional tags are used for several tasks, such as <i> used to italicize the heading, <em> tags are used to surround the keywords since they carry a weight and are important for a research paper, etc. Tags for images and tables are not used since there were none in the chosen paper.

Conclusion

Each HTML tag is carefully selected to ensure relevance and representation of the educational content. The structure is designed to ensure the integrity and presentation of the paper that is easy to read and navigate. Applying these options ensures easy compatibility of the web page with the tool and maintains an accurate, hierarchical representation of the document content.
